Canada Introduction

Canada (English, French: [kanada]) is a country in the northern part of North America, belonging to the Western and Northern hemispheres, its territory bordered by the Pacific Ocean to the west, the Atlantic Ocean to the east, the Arctic Ocean to the north, and part of its territory within the Arctic Circle. Canada borders the Danish territory of Greenland to the northeast and is bordered by Hans Island, Russia across the sea to the north, the United States mainland and Alaska to the south and northwest, and the French possessions of Saint Pierre and Miquelon among its islands to the east. With an area of 9,984,670 square kilometers, Canada is the second largest country in the world and the largest among developed countries. Canada has the longest coastline in the world. The country's capital is Ottawa, and the top five cities in the country by population in 2016; Toronto, Montreal, Vancouver, Calgary, Edmonton.Ten provinces and three territories make up the Confederation of Canada. Canada is included in Forbes' list of best countries to retire in 2020.

Canada History

Canada has been inhabited by Aboriginal people since 1400 years ago. In the late 15th century, English and French colonists began to explore the east coast of North America and establish colonies there.

In 1763, at the end of the Seven Years' War, France ceded almost all of its North American colonies to Britain. In the following decades, the British explored westward into the Pacific and established several new colonies.

On July 1, 1867, the Constitution Act of 1867 was passed, and the Province of Canada was split into the provinces of Ontario and Quebec, joining the three British North American colonies of New Brunswick and Nova Scotia to form the Confederation of Canada. Over the next 100 years, several other British North American colonies joined the Union to form modern Canada

On December 11, 1931, the United Kingdom passed the Act of Westminster, which made Canada an independent country, the head of state was still King George V, and the British Parliament still had the power to amend the Constitution of Canada.

In 1933 and 1949, the power of final adjudication in Canadian civil and criminal cases was transferred from the Judicial Committee of the Privy Council to the Supreme Court of Canada. On March 29, 1982, Queen Elizabeth II of Canada gave Royal Assent to the Parliament of Canada, transferring the power to amend the Constitution of Canada, thus ending the special legislative relationship between Canada and the United Kingdom.
